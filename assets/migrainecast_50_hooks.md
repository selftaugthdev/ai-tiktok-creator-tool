# TikTok Carousel Hooks — Symptoms & Awareness

## Misdiagnosis & identity

1. Migraine is the most underdiagnosed neurological condition on the planet.
   Millions of people have it right now and have been told it's "just a headache."

2. If a doctor ever dismissed your migraine as stress or anxiety — you're not alone.
   And you were probably right to be angry.

3. The average migraine sufferer waits 7 years before getting a correct diagnosis.
   Seven years of being told to drink more water and take paracetamol.

4. Migraine is the second most disabling condition in the world according to the WHO.
   More disabling than diabetes. More disabling than depression. Still called "just a headache."

5. You can have a migraine with zero head pain.
   It's called a silent migraine — and thousands of people have no idea they're experiencing one.

6. Some people go temporarily blind during a migraine.
   It's called a retinal migraine and it's more common than you think.

## Symptoms nobody talks about

7. Your skin hurts during a migraine. Not metaphorically — actually hurts to touch.
   It's called allodynia and it affects over 60% of migraine sufferers.

8. Some people lose the ability to speak mid-sentence during a migraine aura.
   It's not a stroke. It's aphasia — and it's terrifying if you don't know it's coming.

9. The migraine isn't over when the headache stops.
   The postdrome phase leaves you foggy, exhausted, and emotionally flat for up to 24 hours after.

10. Migraine makes some people temporarily see the world as smaller or larger than it really is.
    It's called Alice in Wonderland syndrome. It's a real neurological symptom.

11. Children can get migraines in their stomach — with no head pain at all.
    Abdominal migraine is widely missed because nobody connects the dots.

12. Some people experience a full-body aura before the headache hits.
    Tingling in the hands, face, arms — that's your nervous system, not anxiety.

13. Dizziness so severe you can't stand up — with no headache at all — can be a migraine.
    Vestibular migraine is one of the most common causes of vertigo and one of the least diagnosed.

14. Craving specific foods before a migraine isn't a cause. It's a symptom.
    Your brain is already in the prodrome phase — it's driving the craving, not the other way around.

15. Temporary partial paralysis on one side of the body is a real type of migraine.
    Hemiplegic migraine gets mistaken for stroke in emergency rooms every day.

## The emotional and cognitive reality

16. "Migraine brain" is real. The cognitive fog, memory gaps, and inability to find words —
    that's your brain in a neurological state, not you being dramatic.

17. Migraine doesn't just take hours. It takes days.
    Most attacks have four stages — and the ones before and after the headache are just as brutal.

18. Chronic migraine is defined as 15 or more headache days per month.
    Imagine spending half your life in pain and still being told to just manage your stress.

19. The emotional crash after a migraine — the low mood, the exhaustion, the grief — is physiological.
    It's the postdrome. And nobody explains it to you when you're diagnosed.

20. Migraine affects more people worldwide than diabetes, epilepsy, and asthma combined.
    But it receives a fraction of the research funding.

## Awareness hooks

21. One in seven people has migraine. That's more than one person in every room you walk into.

22. Migraine is three times more common in women than men.
    It is not a personality trait. It is not a coping mechanism. It is biology.

23. You can have an aura without ever having a headache.
    Flashing lights, zigzag lines, blind spots — and then nothing. Still a migraine.

24. The light that triggers your migraine isn't just bright light.
    It's specific wavelengths. Blue and amber light are the worst offenders — not just overall brightness.

25. Most people with chronic migraine also have anxiety or depression.
    It's not because they're weak. They share the same neural pathways.

26. Migraine attacks increase your risk of stroke — but only in specific groups and specific conditions.
    This is something every migraine sufferer deserves to know and discuss with a doctor.

27. The nausea during a migraine isn't a side effect of the pain.
    It's a separate neurological symptom happening in parallel — which is why painkillers alone often don't touch it.

28. Visual snow — seeing static like an old TV screen — is a symptom linked to migraine
    that persists even between attacks. Most sufferers think it's just how their eyes work.

29. Yawning excessively for no reason might not mean you're tired.
    It's one of the earliest prodrome signals — your body warning you hours before the attack hits.

30. People with migraine have a measurably lower pain threshold across their entire body —
    not just their head. It's a systemic neurological condition wearing the mask of a headache.

# 30 TikTok Carousel Hooks — Tips & Tricks (Lesser-Known)

## Supplements & nutrition

1. A study found ginger powder worked as well as sumatriptan for stopping a migraine.
   Most people have it in their kitchen and have never tried it.
   (Source: Phytotherapy Research, 2014)

2. 400mg of vitamin B2 daily was shown to cut migraine frequency nearly in half.
   Almost nobody talks about it. Almost every neurologist knows about it.
   (Source: Neurology, 1998 — replicated multiple times since)

3. Most magnesium supplements do almost nothing for migraines.
   The form you take changes everything. Here's what to look for.
   (Magnesium glycinate/citrate vs oxide — oxide has very low bioavailability)

4. CoQ10 at 300mg/day reduced migraine days by nearly 50% in a controlled trial.
   It's sold at every pharmacy. Almost no one recommends it.
   (Source: Cephalalgia, 2005)

5. Vitamin D deficiency is significantly more common in chronic migraine sufferers.
   Correcting it doesn't just help your bones.
   (Source: Multiple studies, including Headache journal 2020)

6. Melatonin at 3mg before bed was found to reduce migraine frequency in a clinical trial.
   It's not just for sleep.
   (Source: Neurology, 2016)

7. Your gut microbiome is measurably different if you suffer from migraines.
   Researchers are now looking at probiotics as a prevention strategy.
   (Source: Journal of Headache and Pain, 2021)

## Biology & mechanism

8. The real cause of migraine pain isn't in your head. It's in a nerve called the trigeminal.
   Understanding this changes how you treat it.

9. There's a protein your body releases during a migraine attack.
   Scientists spent 30 years targeting it. Now it's the basis of the newest migraine drugs.
   (CGRP — calcitonin gene-related peptide)

10. What actually happens in your brain during a migraine aura is one of the strangest things
    in neuroscience. It's called cortical spreading depression.

11. The reason migraines make you hate light might have nothing to do with your eyes.
    It's a specific photoreceptor — melanopsin — that connects directly to pain pathways.
    (Source: Nature Neuroscience, 2010)

12. Migraine sufferers' brains are structurally different on MRI scans.
    This is a neurological condition with physical evidence — not anxiety, not sensitivity.

13. The prodrome phase starts up to 48 hours before the headache.
    Yawning, food cravings, mood shifts — your body is already signalling.

## Triggers most people miss

14. Weekend migraines aren't bad luck. They're caused by a cortisol crash after a stressful week.
    It's called a "let-down migraine" and it's well documented.

15. Sleeping in on weekends disrupts your circadian rhythm enough to trigger an attack.
    The fix isn't sleeping less — it's sleeping consistently.

16. If you clench your jaw at night, you may be triggering migraines you think are random.
    The TMJ-migraine connection is real and massively underdiagnosed.

17. Aged cheese, red wine, and cured meats contain tyramine.
    But the dose matters more than most people realise — small amounts are usually fine.

18. You don't have to smell something bad to trigger a migraine.
    Any strong smell — even ones you like — can activate the trigeminal nerve.

19. Altitude changes trigger migraines through the same mechanism as barometric pressure.
    Flying, mountain trips, even tall buildings — your brain notices.

20. Histamine in fermented foods (kimchi, kombucha, aged wine) is a hidden trigger for some people.
    It's not an allergy — it's an enzyme deficiency that's easy to test for.

## Treatment & relief hacks

21. Peppermint oil applied to the temples was shown to be as effective as paracetamol
    in reducing tension-related head pain. And it works in minutes.
    (Source: Cephalalgia, 1996)

22. Cold on your head AND heat on your neck at the same time works better than either alone.
    The mechanism is vasodilation vs constriction in complementary areas.

23. The acupressure point between your thumb and index finger (LI-4)
    has been studied for migraine relief and shows measurable results in trials.
    (Source: Pain Medicine, multiple studies)

24. Using painkillers more than 10–15 days a month actually causes more migraines.
    It's called medication overuse headache — and it traps millions of people.

25. Aerobic exercise 3 times a week was found to be as effective as topiramate for migraine prevention.
    In a head-to-head trial. Almost nobody gets told this.
    (Source: Cephalalgia, 2011)

26. A small amount of caffeine early in an attack can help — but only if you don't use it daily.
    Daily caffeine use raises your baseline and makes this trick stop working entirely.

27. Pressing firmly on the greater occipital nerve (base of the skull, slightly off-centre)
    can interrupt a migraine in progress for some people. It's the basis of nerve block injections.

## Patterns & tracking

28. Morning migraines that wake you up are associated with sleep apnea in research.
    If this is a pattern for you, it may be worth a sleep study — not just a new painkiller.

29. Women are 3x more likely to get migraines than men — almost entirely because of oestrogen.
    The drop just before menstruation is one of the most consistent triggers in all of migraine research.

30. Tracking just 3 variables — sleep, stress, and what you ate — is enough to identify
    your personal triggers in under 60 days. Most people never do it. The ones who do
    report dramatically fewer attacks.
